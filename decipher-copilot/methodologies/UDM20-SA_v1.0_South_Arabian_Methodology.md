## Core Position

UDM20-SA documents the methodology for **Old South Arabian / Musnad** (c. 1000 BCE – 600 CE), used by the kingdoms of Saba, Qataban, Hadramaut, and Ma'in in modern Yemen/Oman. The script IS DECIPHERED (consonantal alphabet, 29 letters), and the languages (Sabaic, Qatabanian, Hadramitic, Minaic) are substantially understood. This methodology serves as a reference implementation showing how the UDM20 framework applies to a CONFIRMED SUCCESS case.

## Phase Summary (Completed Decipherment)

### How It Was Done (Historical)
- **Bilingual approach**: Greek-Sabaic bilinguals from trade contexts
- **Comparative Semitic**: South Arabian languages are Semitic — Arabic, Ge'ez, Hebrew parallels
- **Consistent alphabet**: 29 consonant letters, consistent across all texts and periods
- **Massive corpus**: Thousands of monumental inscriptions (well-preserved stone)

### Why It Succeeded
- Language family KNOWN (Semitic — rich comparative data)
- Alphabet simple and consistent (29 letters = 29 consonants, boustrophedon/R→L)
- Long, well-preserved monumental texts (not short seal inscriptions)
- Greek bilinguals provided initial key
- Modern South Arabian languages (Mehri, Soqotri, Jibbali) as living descendants

### UDM20 Phase Mapping (Retrospective)
- Phase 1: Alphabet identified quickly (29 distinct letters, no allographs)
- Phase 2: Semitic comparative linguistics provided immediate language family anchor
- Phase 3: Formulaic inscriptions (building, dedication, military) provided semantic fields
- Phase 4: Grammar reconstructed via Semitic parallels + internal consistency
- Phases 5-20: Completed progressively through the 19th-20th centuries
