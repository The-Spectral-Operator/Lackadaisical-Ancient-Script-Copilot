## Core Position

UDM20-PE targets **Proto-Elamite** (c. 3100–2900 BCE), the earliest writing system of the Iranian Plateau. Unlike most undeciphered scripts, Proto-Elamite has a LARGE corpus (~1,600 tablets) with clear numerical/administrative structure, but the non-numerical signs remain stubbornly undeciphered. The methodology leverages the known numerical system as an anchor.

---

## Why a Proto-Elamite-Specific UDM Is Needed

- The numerical system IS deciphered (multiple metrological systems confirmed)
- Administrative tablet format is understood (accounts, disbursements, inventories)
- ~900 distinct non-numerical signs remain undeciphered
- The underlying language is unknown (possibly related to Elamite, possibly not)
- Heavy front-loading of structure: headers + numerical entries format is clear
- Geographic concentration: primarily Susa, Tepe Yahya, Tal-i Malyan, Sialk

## Phase Adaptations

### PHASE 1: Sign Inventory (Adapted)
- Use Dahl (2005, 2019) sign list and CDLI digital corpus
- ~1,200 distinct signs (many hapax legomena)
- Separate numerical signs (deciphered) from logographic/phonetic signs (undeciphered)
- Identify "header" patterns that precede numerical entries
- Complex sign composition: many signs are combinations of basic elements
- Output: Cleaned sign inventory with composition analysis

### PHASE 2: MEGA-Correlation (Adapted)
- Tier 1 (35% weight): Proto-cuneiform (Uruk IV/III) structural parallels
- Tier 2 (30% weight): Linear Elamite (possible descendant, partial decipherment)
- Tier 3 (20% weight): Later Elamite cuneiform (known language, different script)
- Tier 4 (15% weight): Administrative structure parallels (Mesopotamian bookkeeping)
- **Anchor**: The numerical system provides verified structural framework

### PHASE 3: Semantic Clustering (Adapted)
- Headers = "account titles" (persons, institutions, or commodity categories)
- Numerical entries = quantities of goods (systems: bisexagesimal, decimal, capacity, area)
- Recurrent header signs = likely institutional names or official titles
- Animal/commodity ideograms (some identifiable from context + later Elamite parallels)
- The "balanced account" format: entries sum to totals
- Output: Functional taxonomy of sign positions within administrative framework

### PHASE 4: Structural Analysis (Adapted)
- Text structure is RIGID: header → entries → total format
- This rigidity enables structural analysis even without phonetic readings
- Sign-combination rules (ligatures, determinatives?)
- Can we identify "name" vs. "title" vs. "commodity" slots by position?
- Output: Slot-grammar of administrative format

### PHASE 5-8: Archaeological Integration
- Phase 5: Iranian Plateau economy reconstruction (Susa, highland sites)
- Phase 6: Proto-Elamite expansion period (3100-2900 BCE) and collapse
- Phase 7: Relationship to contemporary proto-cuneiform (parallel invention? borrowing?)
- Phase 8: Seal impressions as verification corpus (match seal owners to tablet entries)

### PHASE 9-12: Decipherment Strategy
- Start from KNOWN: numerical system → commodity identification → institutional identification
- Use balanced accounts to identify commodity signs (X units of COMMODITY = consistent)
- Use header positions to identify title/name signs
- Cross-reference with Linear Elamite (Desset 2022 partial decipherment)
- Test: do Proto-Elamite signs match any Linear Elamite phonetic values?

### PHASE 13-16: Validation
- Any proposed reading must be consistent across ALL tablets where that sign appears
- Numerical cross-checks: do proposed commodity readings generate sensible quantities?
- Geographic consistency: do Susa vs. highland tablets show expected economic differences?
- Chronological layering: early vs. late Proto-Elamite sign variation

### PHASE 17-20: Breakthrough Tracking
- Currently at: ~20% decipherment (numerical system + some logograms)
- Major breakthrough: Desset (2022) Linear Elamite partial decipherment provides back-testing
- Best path: computational analysis of administrative patterns + Linear Elamite bridge
- Watch for: New tablets from ongoing Iranian excavations

## Critical Warnings

1. **Numerical ≠ Linguistic**: Deciphering numbers does NOT automatically decrypt the language
2. **Sign Complexity**: Many signs are composed — distinguish base signs from ligatures
3. **Short Life**: The script was used for only ~200 years before disappearing — limited corpus despite size
4. **Language Unknown**: Do NOT assume it's early Elamite — it might be a different language
5. **Mesopotamian Bias**: Don't over-apply cuneiform administrative logic to a different system
