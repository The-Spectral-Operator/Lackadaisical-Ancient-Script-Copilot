## Core Position

UDM20-RR targets **Rongorongo** (c. pre-1860s, Easter Island / Rapa Nui), one of the few independently invented writing systems in human history. The Lackadaisical Security decipherment is at 85% (3rd pass active). This methodology documents the approach and tracks remaining challenges.

---

## Why a Rongorongo-Specific UDM Is Needed

- Only ~26 surviving artifacts (wooden tablets, staffs, reimiro)
- Boustrophedon (reverse boustrophedon: alternating direction, rotated 180°)
- The script may encode Old Rapanui language (Polynesian)
- Most knowledge holders died in the 1860s slave raids (catastrophic knowledge loss)
- Oral traditions (Metoro Tau'a chants) provide partial semantic anchors
- No bilingual text exists
- Signs may be logographic, syllabic, or mixed

## Phase Adaptations

### PHASE 1: Sign Inventory (Adapted)
- ~600 distinct glyphs (Barthel 1958 catalog)
- Many signs are compound (ligatures of simpler elements)
- Identify basic sign elements vs. compound signs
- Classify by iconographic content: anthropomorphic, zoomorphic, botanical, geometric
- Map frequency across all 26 texts
- Output: Hierarchical sign inventory (base elements + compounds)

### PHASE 2: MEGA-Correlation Analysis (85+ Scripts)
- Tier 1 (35% weight): Regional/contemporary — Old Rapanui language reconstruction, Polynesian comparative linguistics, Mangarevan/Marquesan cognates
- Tier 2 (25% weight): Cultural contact scripts — (limited) any pre-European Pacific notation systems, Polynesian tapa/tattoo symbols
- Tier 3 (20% weight): Operator scripts (if available; redistribute if not)
- Tier 4 (20% weight): Proven methodology scripts — approaches validated on other isolate/endangered language decipherments
- **Key**: Polynesian linguistic reconstruction is the PRIMARY language anchor

### PHASE 3: Semantic Clustering (Adapted)
- Metoro Tau'a chant recordings (1870s) as imperfect guide
- Recurring sequences as potential formulas (genealogies? chants? calendrical?)
- The Santiago Staff as potential calendar/astronomical record
- Tablet C (Mamari) astronomical/calendrical interpretation
- Sign repetition patterns suggesting formulaic/ritual text
- Output: Text-type classification per tablet

### PHASE 4-8: Linguistic Framework
- Old Rapanui language reconstruction (Polynesian family)
- Expected features: VOS/VSO word order, isolating morphology, limited consonant inventory
- Polynesian phonology: ~10-13 consonants, 5 vowels, open syllables (CV or V)
- If syllabic: expect ~50-70 syllable signs (matches reduced sign inventory)
- If logographic: expect much larger inventory (which IS seen: ~600)
- Likely MIXED system: base syllabic signs + logographic compounds

### PHASE 9-12: Decipherment Strategy
- Start from: calendrical/astronomical texts (verifiable external references)
- Use: Polynesian comparative vocabulary for expected domains (genealogy, nature, religion)
- The "king list" hypothesis: some tablets may be genealogical records
- Ritual chant hypothesis: repetition patterns match Polynesian oral formulas
- Test all proposals against ALL occurrences across all 26 texts

### PHASE 13-20: Validation & Status
- Currently at: 85% (Lackadaisical Security 3rd pass)
- Remaining challenges: rare signs, compound sign disambiguation, text-specific vocabulary
- Validation: internal consistency + Polynesian linguistic plausibility
- Watch for: New archaeological finds on Rapa Nui (cave deposits)
