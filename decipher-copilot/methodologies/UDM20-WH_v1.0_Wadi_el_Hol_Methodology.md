## Core Position

UDM20-WH targets the **Wadi el-Hol inscriptions** (c. 1900–1800 BCE), two rock-cut graffiti from the Egyptian Western Desert that may represent the earliest alphabetic writing — predating Proto-Sinaitic by centuries. These inscriptions bridge Egyptian hieroglyphs and the Proto-Sinaitic/Proto-Canaanite alphabetic tradition.

This methodology is a companion to the completed **Proto-Sinaitic Decipherment** (Lackadaisical Security, ~95%).

## Phase Adaptations

### PHASE 1: Sign Inventory
- Two short inscriptions (~12-15 characters total between them)
- Signs clearly derived from Egyptian hieroglyphs (acrophonic principle)
- Several signs match Proto-Sinaitic forms
- The "ox-head" (ʾaleph), "house" (beth), "throw-stick" (gimel) identifiable
- Context: military road between Thebes and Abydos
- Output: Sign-by-sign comparison with Egyptian sources + Proto-Sinaitic descendants

### PHASE 2: MEGA-Correlation Analysis (85+ Scripts)
- Tier 1 (35% weight): Regional/contemporary — Egyptian hieroglyphs (source script), Proto-Sinaitic (near-identical system), Proto-Canaanite (early descendant)
- Tier 2 (25% weight): Cultural contact scripts — Byblos syllabary (deciphered by LS), early Northwest Semitic writing, Serabit el-Khadim inscriptions
- Tier 3 (20% weight): Operator scripts (if available; redistribute if not)
- Tier 4 (20% weight): Proven methodology scripts — LS Proto-Sinaitic decipherment as direct framework
- **Key**: Proto-Sinaitic (95% deciphered by LS) provides the PRIMARY reading framework — Wadi el-Hol is likely the same or ancestral system

### PHASE 3: Reading Attempt
- Apply Proto-Sinaitic sign values directly (most signs match)
- Expected language: early Northwest Semitic (Canaanite)
- Context suggests: Semitic-speaking workers/soldiers on Egyptian military road
- Short texts likely: names, simple phrases, or dedications
- Acrophonic principle from Egyptian originals constrains values
- Output: Proposed readings with per-sign confidence

### PHASE 4-8: Historical Context
- Middle Kingdom Egypt (12th Dynasty): heavy Semitic population in Egypt
- Military road context: expeditionary forces included Semitic auxiliaries
- The invention of the alphabet: Wadi el-Hol may BE the earliest example
- Relationship to Serabit el-Khadim mining inscriptions (same workers?)
- Egyptian hieroglyphic source signs as confirmed starting point

### PHASE 9-12: Alphabetic Origin Theory
- Test acrophonic origin for each sign (Egyptian word → first consonant)
- Cross-validate with Proto-Sinaitic (same signs, slightly later)
- The "alphabet was invented ONCE" hypothesis gets strongest test here
- Historical linguistics of early Canaanite as phonological constraint

### PHASE 13-20: Status
- Currently at: ~60-70% (via Proto-Sinaitic parallel; some signs clear, some debated)
- Barrier: Only 2 very short texts — limited by corpus size
- Best path: Direct application of LS Proto-Sinaitic framework
- Watch for: Additional early alphabetic inscriptions from Egyptian desert roads
